Banking

Banking information



Financial institutions in the United States offer many kinds of financial services, including:

  • Checking and savings accounts
  • Foreign currency conversion
  • Bank drafts
  • Money orders
  • Credit cards
  • Loans
  • Interbank transfer
  • Traveler’s checks
  • Safe-deposit boxes for storing valuables
  • Investment services

Chase

JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A., doing business as Chase, is a national bank that constitutes the consumer and commercial banking subsidiary of the multinational banking corporation JPMorgan Chase. Most IU students opt for this bank since it has the most number of ATMs located on the campus. It also provides most of the services mentioned above. If you are above 25 in age, then you need to maintain a minimum balance of $2500 in your Chase account. Check out more details here.


IU Credit Union

IU Credit Union is another one of the favored banks among IU students. IU Credit Union is the eighth largest credit union in Indiana. As a credit union, it has most of the same functions as a bank, but with generally lower fees and higher returns to the customer. It is, as most credit unions are, effectively a co-op.
